Business Etiquette

In a business environment, it is essential to demonstrate proper etiquette{XE "etiquette"}. This means knowing the correct way to act in any setting. Adhering to proper business etiquette improves your professional image and may help further your career. Conversely, failing to act appropriately can damage your career and eliminate opportunities for advancement. Following proper business etiquette also presents a professional impression to your clients, colleagues, and supervisors. It is important to understand the proper way to conduct yourself in a business setting.

Interactions with Others

You should be aware several areas of that require proper etiquette{XE "etiquette"} when interacting with others. These areas include greeting associates, introducing people, and conversations.

Greetings{XE "Greetings"}

Shaking hands{XE "Shaking hands"} is the most accepted way to greet associates you already know. In addition, you should always shake hands with someone when introduced to them for the first time. Handshakes should be firm. If possible, you should stand when shaking hands. In certain situations, hugs{XE "Greetings:hugs"} are also an acceptable form of a greeting.

Introductions

When introducing people, introduce people of lower rank to people of higher rank. Remember to include both the first and last names{XE "names"} in the introduction. Try to provide some information about each person as you introduce them, as well as titles{XE "titles"} and name prefixes{XE "name prefix"}.

Conversations

When involved in a conversation with business associates there are several points to keep in mind. You should always let the person speaking complete his or her comments before speaking yourself—never interrupt. Always show interest in what others have to say, keep topics{XE "topics"} on areas of mutual interest, maintain eye contact{XE "eye:contact"} with the person speaking, and involve everyone in the discussion. Table Manners

Business lunches and dinners require proper table manners. Table manners include knowing what to do with your napkin{XE "napkin"}, understanding the place setting, and knowing what to do (and not do) when eating{XE "eating"} the meal.

Napkin

After everyone at your table is seated, you should unfold your napkin and place it in your lap. Your napkin{XE "napkin"} should remain in your lap the entire meal. If you do leave the table during the meal, place your napkin on your chair. At the end of the meal, you should place the napkin on the table next to your plate. Never use a napkin as a tissue.

Place Setting

All items in a place setting have traditional locations. Silverware{XE "Silverware"} is placed around the dinner plate—forks{XE "Silverware:forks"} to the left, knives{XE "Silverware:knives"} and spoons{XE "Silverware:spoons"} to the right, and dessert utensils (if included) above. Use the silverware from the outside moving in towards the dinner plate. Bread or salad plates are to the left of the dinner plate, and glasses are to the right. Eating the Meal

Do not start eating{XE "eating"} until everyone at your table has been served. If there is a delay in serving some guests, these guests may encourage you to begin without them. In this situation, you may begin eating. When you have finished your meal, place your fork and knife on the side of the plate to indicate to the servers that you have finished.

You should always chew with your mouth closed. You should never talk with food in your mouth, or do anything that makes loud noises at the table. You should not put your elbows{XE "elbows"} on the table, although you may lean your forearms against the table. You should never reach across someone for an item on the table; always ask someone to pass the item.
